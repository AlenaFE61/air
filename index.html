Types of air conditioners:

  • PORTABLE AIR CONDITIONERS

    Portable air conditioners, also known as monobloc or self-contained units, are ideal for cooling single rooms. They are easy to move between rooms thanks to their wheels and do not require permanent installation. These units have an attached hose that expels hot air outside through a nearby window. They are a great option when permanent installation is not feasible due to space constraints or building restrictions.

  • SPLIT AIR CONDITIONERS

    Split air conditioning systems consist of two main components: an outdoor condensing unit and an indoor evaporator. These units are quieter and more efficient than portable models but require professional installation. The outdoor unit contains a compressor that circulates refrigerant, which absorbs heat from the indoor air and releases it outside. The indoor unit blows cooled air into the room.

Types of heat pumps:

  • AIR SOURCE HEAT PUMPS

    Air source heat pumps extract heat from the outside air and transfer it into your home or office to provide heating. They work efficiently even in cold climates, making them a cost-effective and environmentally friendly heating solution.

  • GROUND SOURCE HEAT PUMPS

    Ground source heat pumps use pipes buried underground to extract heat from the ground. They are highly efficient and can provide both heating and cooling, making them a versatile choice for residential and commercial buildings.

Types of fire dampers:

  • MECHANICAL VENTILATION

    This system uses fans to exhaust stale air and introduce fresh air into the building. We can install and maintain various types of mechanical ventilation systems, including heat recovery ventilation (HRV) and energy recovery ventilation (ERV) systems, which help reduce energy costs by recovering heat or coolness from the exhaust air.

  • NATURAL VENTILATION

    We also provide installation and servicing for natural ventilation systems, which rely on natural airflow and pressure differences to ventilate a building. Properly designed natural ventilation can improve indoor air quality and reduce the need for mechanical ventilation.

  • MIXED-MODE VENTILATION

    This system combines natural and mechanical ventilation strategies to optimize airflow and energy efficiency. Our experts can design and install mixed-mode ventilation systems tailored to your building's specific requirements.

  • VENTILATION SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
